Virtual Production Places

~1 min 34 sec · 3 weeks · Bond University · group short film · LED virtual production

Shot with a motion-tracked LED volume: the in-camera display tracks perspective so the Unreal environment reads as a next-generation green screen behind the performer.

WHAT IT IS

Virtual Production Places is a Bond University group multimedia short set in an abandoned underwater facility. The production combined a physical performer on set with a real-time Unreal Engine environment played back on the LED wall.

I led the digital build and pipeline for the project — environment, animation, lighting, renders, and on-set scene setup — while coordinating progress with the team through our Microsoft Teams production chat.

MY CONTRIBUTIONS

  • Built the entire 3D Unreal environment for the facility and supporting locations
  • Authored all Blueprint animation (self-taught): flickering lights, shark motion, water ripples, jellyfish bobbing
  • Handled in-engine lighting and rendered every shot where the actor was not immediately in frame
  • Modelled and integrated a carpark into the world — mesh edits and supporting VFX
  • Prepared the film-day setup (location, angle, scene state) and worked with Nirav to resolve on-set technical issues
  • Documented progress continuously in the group Teams chat so the team had a clear record of digital delivery
  • Delivered a hard-drive copy of the finished Unreal scene after the team agreed to render additional shots themselves
  • Advocated for a submission extension when the group was blocked by technical problems and risked handing in an incomplete cut
  • Personally sourced Unreal marketplace assets (including the shark) to meet scope under time pressure — purchased voluntarily, not billed to the team

TIME & SCOPE

The project ran for three weeks at roughly six hours per day on my side for the digital work, with an intensive earlier pass at about five hours daily over two weeks while the environment and animation systems came together.

That effort covered environment art, Blueprint systems, lighting, offline renders, carpark integration, and on-set support — not just a single hero asset.

TECHNICAL FOCUS

  • LED / ICVFX workflow: plates and silhouettes that read on camera
  • Atmosphere, scale, and materials tuned for cinematic blocking
  • Practical-to-digital handoff for performer integration on the volume
