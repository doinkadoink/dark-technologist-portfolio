Underwater facility

1:34 · Group short film · Bond University · LED volume & motion-tracked screen

Virtual production method: a large LED screen with motion-tracked perspective — acting as a next-generation green screen so the camera and performer share the same parallax as the Unreal environment.

WHAT IT IS

A multimedia short film set in an abandoned underwater facility, produced as a Bond University virtual production assignment. The digital world was built in Unreal Engine and played back on set through an LED volume so live action could be captured in-camera against a reactive 3D backdrop.

MY CONTRIBUTIONS

Reflection on my role on the digital side of the project (progress was also documented continuously in our team Microsoft Teams chat).

  • Full Unreal environment — built the entire 3D underwater facility in Unreal Engine.
  • Blueprint animation (self-taught) — lights flickering, shark movement, water rippling, jellyfish bobbing.
  • Lighting & rendering — in-game lighting; created and rendered every scene that did not have the actor immediately in shot.
  • On-set support — prepared the scene for film day, organised location and camera angle, and worked with Nirav to resolve technical issues on the floor.
  • Carpark extension — modelled carpark geometry for integration into the environment, edited the mesh, and handled related VFX.
  • Production leadership — encouraged the team to request an extension when technical blockers risked an incomplete submit; handed off a hard-drive copy of the scene after the team agreed to render additional passes (those passes were not completed by others).
  • Time & resources — roughly two weeks at ~5 hours per day on the digital work; personally purchased $100+ of Unreal marketplace assets (including the shark) to meet ambition and deadlines — my own choice, not billed to the team.
